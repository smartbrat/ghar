Imagery

Photography.
Warm, real, uncluttered.

Natural light. Indian or relatable Asian faces. Real interiors and architecture. Photos lead without overlays — the image is the story. Shapes and the G mark are used on photography only in print, merchandise, and editorial contexts.

Photography Direction
Warm, natural light. Indian or relatable Asian faces when human subjects appear. Real interiors, real architecture — no stock-generic. Property photos use 4:3 aspect; heroes use 16:9; discovery grids use 1:1. When Pexels cannot provide confirmed Indian faces, use no-face alternatives: property aerials, city skylines, material close-ups.

sounds.

Restraint

Shapes support.
They don't dominate.

The nine shapes are a vocabulary, not a wallpaper. Use them at roughly 5% of any page view — one per section, never all at once. Ghar.tv must look diverse, not monolithic.

Avoid — Overloaded

Homes worth talking about.

When shapes appear in every heading, every card, every corner, the brand becomes monotone. The shapes lose their power and the platform stops looking diverse.

Preferred — Restrained

Homes worth talking about.

One shape per section. Let whitespace, typography, and photography do the heavy lifting. The shape becomes a punctuation mark — not the sentence.

01
The 5% Rule
Shapes occupy roughly 5% of any given page view — the same discipline as brand red. They accent, not dominate.
02
One Per Section
A section can have one inline shape, OR a masked photo, OR an accent mark. Never all three. Pick the single most impactful use.
03
Diversity Over Identity
Ghar.tv must look like a diverse platform, not "the shapes brand." Content, typography, and photography carry the identity — shapes support it.

03 — Shape as glyph

The house is a word.
Not an icon. Not a logo. A letter in the alphabet.

The nine canonical shapes aren't decoration — they're typography. They live inside sentences, prefix category labels, open quotations, anchor data. Any place a word can go, a shape can go. The reader reads them without noticing they aren't letters.

Exhibit A — Shape as noun
Home is where Maya, her little son laughs, and the afternoon light settles on the wooden floor.
The shape replaces the word. Three houses read as three rooms, three ways a home holds a moment. The eye doesn't trip — it glides through the sentence.
Exhibit B — Shape as category mark
Editorial GharTalks GharEvents VideoWorks Community
The five sections that earn a color each get their own shape and tone — together forming a navigational grammar. Warm-paper sections inherit the neutral ink glyph.
Exhibit C — Shape as quotation
I walked into this apartment and knew it was home before I crossed the threshold. The light did all the talking.
Shilpa, Pune — on finding her first home
The house-outline replaces the curly quote mark. Same grammatical function, deeper meaning — the frame of a home now opens the frame of a memory.
Exhibit D — Shape as window
A two-bedroom apartment in Bandra where the mornings smell of filter coffee, and the sea is eight minutes away on foot.
The shape becomes a window. Photography fills the house-silhouette and lives mid-sentence. Listing copy becomes a short story.

06 — The rules for scaling the voice

Eight principles.
If a decision breaks any one of them, the decision is wrong.

These aren't style guidelines. They're the rules that keep the voice coherent across sixteen sections, six card types, B2C and B2B audiences, and years of future content. When you're three screens deep into a layout decision and can't choose — come back here.

01
Warm paper is the canvas.
Body is #faf7f2 everywhere — product surfaces too. Pure white is a deliberate object that floats on the paper (inner cards, search bars, pills). If a section needs pure white, it's wrong.
02
The house is a glyph.
The nine canonical shapes are typography. They live inside sentences, prefix categories, open quotes, anchor data. Never decoration. Never icons. If a shape isn't doing the work of a letter, cut it.
03
Color is vocabulary — earned, not assigned.
Indigo Mist for Editorial (written ink), Espresso for GharTalks (listening room), Turmeric for GharEvents (ceremony), Ink for VideoWorks (cinema), Terracotta for Community (earth of people). Five earned tones — none repeated, none adjacent. If you can't defend a color choice semantically in one sentence, and the section next to it isn't warm paper, it doesn't belong.
04
Stories, not specs.
Write every line for the person reading, not the business speaking. "5 new listings match your filter" becomes "a new home story starts with five matches." This is not word-play — it's stance.
05
The product is a polite guest.
Search bars sit next to fireplaces. Notifications speak softly. The interface participates in the room — it doesn't yank you out of it. If a component interrupts the mood, rewrite the component.
06
Data is typography.
Big serif stats. Shape-silhouettes as data dots. Histograms, not rainbow pie charts. Intelligence without clinical. Assume the reader can read a chart — don't dumb the data down to marketing.
07
B2B earns authority through craft.
Broker, developer, and partner sections keep the warm voice but swap emotion for precision. Sharper type hierarchy, tighter grids, denser proof. Never colder color — just tighter composition. Atmosphere stays; editorial shifts.
08
One voice. Sixteen rooms.
Variety lives within the voice, not against it. Different sections have different weight, density, color, and rhythm — but the reader should never feel they left Ghar.tv. If a section feels like it belongs to a different brand, it does. Rebuild it.
A homepage isn't a screen. It's an opening chapter.
Build every section as if the reader will stop here and read to the end.

09 — Micro-UI

Every tooltip is a sentence.
Every toast, a tiny story.

10 — B2B flex

Authority without cold.
The warm voice adapts for brokers and developers — it doesn't switch brands.

B2B pages are where most warm-voice platforms crack and turn into SaaS dashboards. Grey panels. Blue CTAs. Stock icons. On Ghar.tv, the flex works differently: tighter hierarchy, denser proof, precise type — but the paper stays warm and the serif keeps reading. Authority comes from composition, not from colder color.
