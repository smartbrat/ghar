/design vertical — proposed architecture

Move /design from a category-driven model to a tag-driven editorial model: 3 record types, 6 tag dimensions, 5 disciplines + Series + Cities as the navigation surface.

Why we're changing it

The current /design nav mixes four different dimensions in one list:

  • Disciplines — Architecture, Interiors
  • People — Designers
  • Formats — Home Tours, Guides, Featured Designs, Celebrity Homes
  • Topics — Vastu, Materials

Every new business angle wants a new slot. The list grows unbounded. It also can't cleanly absorb the long-tail businesses we already want to cover — visualization studios, switch brands, ceramic studios, lighting designers, art galleries — without adding more nav items.

The model — 3 records, 6 tag dimensions

3 record types are the only things that own data:

RecordURL
Brand/brands/{slug}
Person/people/{slug}
Story/design/{slug} — flat under /design, no extra path segment

6 tag dimensions — controlled vocabulary, CMS-enforced, never free text:

1. Discipline  ·  2. Format  ·  3. Material / Medium  ·  4. Style  ·  5. Region  ·  6. Audience

Every surface on /design/* is a saved tag query rendered with editorial layout. Records live once; they surface on many pages via tags.

Nav labels — consumer-friendly, not industry jargon

Primary — 5 disciplines:

Architecture  ·  Interiors  ·  Art  ·  Furniture & Décor  ·  Spaces

Secondary:

Series  ·  Cities

Five disciplines is the line we hold. Adding a 6th re-opens category sprawl.

URL pattern

Page typeURL
Editorial hub/design
Discipline hubs/design/architecture, /design/interiors, /design/art, /design/furniture-decor, /design/spaces
Series/design/series/{slug} — Home Tours, Studio Visit, Celebrity Homes, India Design 100, Behind the Build
City/design/city/{slug}
Material / Style/design/material/{slug}, /design/style/{slug}
Brands / People slices/design/brands, /design/people
Individual Story/design/{slug}

Discipline / series / city / material / style / brands / people slugs are reserved at the CMS level — story slugs can't collide with them.

How service-to-design businesses fit (Studio FOV example)

Service-to-design businesses — visualization studios, photographers, model-makers, drafting consultants — are not end-design disciplines, so they don't get a discipline hub.

They live as:

  • /brands/{slug} — permanent home, editorial mentions accumulate here over time
  • /people/{founder} — founder profile
  • Surface across /design via tagged Stories appearing on Series (Studio Visit, Behind the Build) and on Discipline hubs that match their work tags
  • Discoverable across other Ghar.tv surfaces: /developers (vendor network), GharTalks, Industry Voices, Brand Connect, GharEvents

No new top-level surface needed. /brands becomes filterable by tag dimension so a developer can browse /brands/visualization-studios (a saved tag query) without us adding a directory vertical.

What this gives us

  • One Story surfaces on 4–5 pages via tags, with no duplication.
  • The nav stays at 5 + 2 no matter how many businesses or content angles we cover.
  • Brand profiles compound — editorial mentions, podcast appearances, rankings all flow programmatically onto /brands/{slug} and /people/{slug}.
  • Long-tail SEO via /design/material/marble, /design/style/indo-modern etc. — every tag becomes a landing page.
  • Sales narrative — /partner/design shows prospects the discovery paths their tags will unlock across the platform.

The one rule that protects it

Tag vocabulary is CMS-controlled. Mergeable, deprecatable, aliasable — never free-text. This is the single piece of governance that keeps the model from rotting. If we let editors invent tags ad-hoc, we're back to category sprawl in 12 months.
