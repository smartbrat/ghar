1. Why this exists

The earlier /design nav mixed four different axes in one list (Disciplines, People, Formats, Topics). Every new business angle wanted a new slot. The list grew unbounded and couldn't cleanly absorb the long-tail brands we already wanted to cover — model-makers, switch brands, ceramic studios, lighting designers, art galleries, acoustics specialists — without adding more nav items.

The fix: stop thinking in categories. Think in records + tags. Every surface becomes a saved tag query rendered with editorial layout. This is exactly the Architectural Digest pattern, verified by going through ad.com directly.

AD's 3 findings that grounded this:

  • AD has only ~5 top-level surfaces in nav (Architecture+Design, Shopping, Celebrity, AD It Yourself, AD PRO). They never let categories proliferate.
  • Stories use flat URLs (/story/{slug}). No tree. The article is one object; tags decide which surface it appears on.
  • The AD PRO Directory has only 4 categories. Everything else is editorial lists (“Maximalist Interior Designers”, “California Landscape Architects”) — curated tag queries with editorial framing. The directory stays small; the list pages do the heavy lifting.

2. The data model — 3 records, never more

RecordURLWhat it is
Brand/brands/{slug}Companies / studios / ateliers / manufacturers / galleries. ONE record per brand. Permanent microsite that compounds with every editorial mention.
Person/people/{slug}Founders / architects / designers / artists / principals. ONE record. Personal-brand layer; aggregates appearances over time.
Story/design/{slug}Editorial content — interview, feature, photo essay, walkthrough, opinion. Flat URL under /design. Linked to the Brand(s) and Person(s) it features.

Every other category of business that appears (model-makers, switches, art galleries, lighting designers, acoustics specialists, ceramic studios, walkthrough services) becomes a Brand + Person record with appropriate tags. No new schema, no new folder.

4. Page types — 6 max

Every URL under /design is a saved tag query rendered with editorial layout. There are exactly 6 page-type patterns. Pages can multiply infinitely; page types stay at 6.

Page typeURL pattern
Editorial hub/design
Pillar hub (one tag query each)/design/architecture, /design/interiors, /design/spaces, /design/designers, /design/vastu, /design/guides, /design/series
Series (curated story sets)/design/series/{slug}
City (region tag query)/design/city/{slug}
Material / Style (long-tail tag landings)/design/material/{slug}, /design/style/{slug}
Individual Story/design/{slug}

Slugs are reserved at the CMS layer: story slugs can’t collide with architecture, interiors, spaces, designers, vastu, guides, series, city, material, style, brands, collections, people. CMS enforces this on story creation.

STRICT — Card eyebrow rule on /design

Every editorial card eyebrow MUST be one of the 7 nav items (optionally with a · sub-tag for Series / Vastu / Guides / Spaces). Examples:

  • Architecture
  • Interiors
  • Series · Studio Visit
  • Vastu · Principles
  • Guides · Materials
  • Spaces · Restaurants

NEVER eyebrow with city alone, or category · city. City moved out of eyebrows entirely.

6. Collections — the cross-cutting lens layer

Collections sit between the pillars and individual stories. They are cross-cutting saved tag queries with editorial framing — the AD “editorial list” pattern (“Maximalist Interior Designers”, “California Landscape Architects”) applied to Ghar.tv's tag system.

Collections live in Act 3 of the locked 8-act page architecture — right after the hero and subnav, before the long editorial stretch. They expose the cross-cutting lenses early so the reader sees the full set of browse intents before diving in.

The on-page rail (8 chips, curated)

The rail shows the 8 most prominent collection lenses at any time — editorial discretion. New collections are added to the modal first; promotion to the rail requires displacing one. The rail caps at 8.

Derived from the 7-pillar IA and the real 60+ brand pool (see docs/BRANDS-curated.md), the current rail is:

  1. Studio Visit → /design/series/studio-visit
  2. Material Stories → /design/series/material-stories
  3. Behind the Build → /design/series/behind-the-build
  4. Home Tours → /design/series/home-tours
  5. Lighting Design → /design/material/lighting (anchored by NEJÉ, Pride, Changi, LightForms, Lux Temporis, Havells)
  6. Stone & Cladding → /design/material/stone-cladding (anchored by Stonelite, Specta, Vibrant Technik, MCM Flexi)
  7. Acoustics & Workplace → /design/series/acoustics-workplace (anchored by Unidus, Muura, Studio 19, Studioworks)
  8. Indian Crafts → /design/style/indian-crafts (anchored by Jaakhi, House of Edwa, Hobo Houz, Venjara Carpets, Fazo Project)

The “See all collections” modal

The full taxonomy lives in a modal triggered by the “See all collections” button on the rail. Modal is the right surface up to ~20 collections; past that, swap to a dedicated /design/collections page (intro copy + featured imagery per axis). The rail never changes — only the “See all” affordance does.

9. Service-to-design businesses (Studio FOV pattern)

Service-to-design businesses — visualization studios, photographers, model-makers, drafting consultants — are not end-design disciplines, so they don’t get a discipline hub. They live as:

  • /brands/{slug} — permanent home, editorial mentions accumulate here over time
  • /people/{founder} — founder profile
  • Surface across /design via tagged Stories appearing on Series (Studio Visit, Behind the Build) and on Pillar hubs that match their work tags
  • Discoverable across other Ghar.tv surfaces: /developers (vendor network), GharTalks, Industry Voices, Brand Connect, GharEvents

10. Sales / media kit (/partner/design)

What your rep shows a prospect (e.g. a model-making studio, anchor switches brand, lighting designer, art gallery). One self-contained page:

  1. Their forever Brand/Person microsite — visual of /brands/{their-name} + /people/{founder}. Owned forever, compounds with every Ghar.tv mention.
  2. Which /design/* surfaces their tags unlock — a tag map showing how their 4–6 tags route them to 8–15 surfaces automatically.
  3. Editorial pathways — Studio Visit · Behind the Build · GharTalks guest · Material Stories feature · India Design 100 candidacy. Programs they can be considered for.
  4. Brand Connect upgrades — VideoWorks production · sponsored Story · featured placement · cross-platform amplification. Capabilities + audience value only, NO internal packaging language.
  5. Single CTA: “Talk to Ghar.tv” → enquiry → marketing team scopes 1:1.

This kit doubles as the public Brand Connect for Design landing page AND the sales deck.

11. The one rule that protects the system

Tag vocabulary is CMS-controlled. Mergeable, deprecatable, aliasable — never free-text.

If we let editors invent tags ad-hoc, we’re back to category sprawl in 12 months. This is the one piece of governance that protects the model long-term.

If anyone proposes a 7th object, an 8th tag dimension, or a category folder — that’s the moment the system starts to rot. AD has held this line for 20 years. We can too.
