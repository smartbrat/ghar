Never force brand shapes or theme colors onto elements where they don't have contextual meaning. Shapes should only appear where they make semantic sense.

**Why:** User rejected house shapes on eco cards (they replaced meaningful illustrations) and brand-blush focus border on search bar (it was fine monochrome). "It should have a meaning or sense depending on where it is being placed."

**How to apply:**
- Upgrade existing design elements to follow the warm theme (e.g. shift `#111` to `#1a1714`) rather than replacing them with entirely new elements
- Search bar and elements on white backgrounds keep dark/monochrome shadows — don't add warm-colored shadow tints or borders where the original was neutral
- No border-top accent bars anywhere — user explicitly hates that design pattern
- Don't use shapes as random decorative fillers — they must relate to their context
- The eco card illustrations were contextually meaningful (house=buyers, profile=brokers, buildings=developers, network=brand partners) — keep that meaning, just warm the tones
